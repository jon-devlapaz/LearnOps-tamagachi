# UX Framework (canonical)

## Summary

`docs/product/ux-framework.md` is the binding UX contract. It governs the metacognitive UX lens, the three-phase loop rationale (with cited cognitive-science evidence including Kornell-Hays-Bjork pretesting, RIF temporal dynamics, and Cepeda spacing), the four node states (locked/primed/drilled/solidified), AI scaffolding contracts, attribution management, framing-and-attrition rules, session guardrails (25-min cap, 4-node cap, 3-retrieval ceiling), reward and sensory feedback (success-dependent, calibrated to cognitive load, inverted-U per Kao 2020), trajectory contrast via the spark→link→chain→clear→tetris band system, the dungeon-map primary metaphor, the Tetris secondary emotional analogy, and the ethical engagement boundary.

Hard invariants: no streaks in MVP; the AI must talk less than the learner during drill; sensory celebration only on success and silent on `drilled`; cold attempt explicitly unscored; mastery bar never lowers to resolve deadlock; ADHD/working-memory accommodations are structural defaults, not toggles.

Includes a detailed Evidence Posture section distinguishing directly supported, indirectly supported, and product-hypothesis claims — the load-bearing artifact for any future scientific-claim audit.

Closes with a 14-question evaluation checklist for any new feature.
